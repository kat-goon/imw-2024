Reading 3: My website is a shifting house next to a river of knowledge. What could yours be?, Laurel Schwulst

Why does Laurel Schwulst advocate for individual websites over social media? Do you agree with her? Please explain your answer.

Laurel Schwulst advocates for individual websites because popular social media sites prioritize advertising over useability. I strongly agree with Schwulst’s advocacy towards individual websites and sentiment towards social media. Since these sites cater towards capitalism, as Schwult’s writing states, “private companies prioritize advertising above the users, happiness is not a priority.” With an individualized website, you, the creator, are in control of how your branding is shown and how your users interact with your website. You also own everything you contribute to your website. Whereas if you were to post on social media, the layout of your work is predetermined, it is harder to show what you contribute as an individual, and the user flow is determined by what will put the most money in the company’s pocket. Individuality allows room for expansion of what we are used to seeing on the web; it allows for growth and novel creation. To solely rely on social media, a predetermined platform that prioritizes advertising over user-based content is to remove your voice and the feelings people feel when interacting with your creation.
