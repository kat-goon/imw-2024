Project 3: Interaction

Introduction

With Project 3, we were given the task of creating an abstract website that is focused on a predetermined interaction. I was assigned the “click” interaction made me think about my childhood. Reminiscing back to my childhood of running around outside with friends during recess and coming home to the computer where I would play flash games made me think about how far the web has come. I especially remember playing dress-up games on Barbie.com and Penguin Diner where I ran a restaurant in the Arctic skating from customer to customer with a click of the mouse. This memory made me want to centralize Project 3 on the nostalgia of my early Internet experience and create a collage as an homage to what was.

Research & Process

To start off the project, I looked into what exactly “click” is. Clicking is one of the most simple yet effective interactions people experience with devices. It quite literally takes the user where they want to go on their respective device. The click interaction navigates users and makes the need to remember complicated keyboard combinations unnecessary. With this in mind, I wanted to create an interaction that navigates the user but also brings in the early 2000s internet experience. This led me to create a collage comprised of 2000s motifs where the user must click to view the final collage.

Developing the aesthetic, my research led me to a lot of imagery that was made from pixels and the Windows XP operating system. This made me want to incorporate iconography into the collage and gave me the idea of using windows to show each image of the collage. I created my own icons to be flat and comprised of "pixels." I chose to go with the colour hot pink to make it relevant to our current year, 2024. This is so that while a majority of the aesthetics are from the past, there is still an element of the 2020s, the decade that this site was created.

People are used to seeing an icon and clicking it. With this interaction, the user will click on the icon and an image will appear and another icon will appear. The user then has to click on the next icon which will again open another image and icon. This simple interaction is the ethos of the click: navigation. This will process will repeat until the user is ultimately met with a nostalgic collage.

While iterating, I decided to remove the windows part of the imagery to look more like a collage and have more interaction between the different elements.

The Final & Reflection

I would describe the final design as a Swiss aesthetic with an avant-garde colour palette. Using bright colours grabs the attention of the viewer immediately, bringing the focus to what the event is. The minimalist aesthetic reflects the education that many design institutions teach. This project really helped me understand flexbox more by testing the limitations. Going forward, I want to push myself more so that the websites I code are more interactive. I struggled with pushing this aspect of the page because I was so focused on maintaining Swiss Style. Making it more interactive would also push my coding abilities, deepening my knowledge and expanding my possibilities.
